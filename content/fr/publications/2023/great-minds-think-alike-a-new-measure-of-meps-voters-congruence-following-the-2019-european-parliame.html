A new measure of MEPs–voters congruence following the 2019 European Parliament elections design: full_width: true toc: present: false offcanvas: false hal: docid: "4124809" url: "https://shs.hal.science/halshs-04124809v1" abstract: >- Do citizens and Members of the European Parliament agree on the format and future of the EU? While the literature has emphasized the gap between pro-European elites and increasingly Eurosceptic electorates, this article relies on a novel dataset to explore the implications of measures of such (in)congruence at the EU level. We compare the preferences of elected representatives and of voters on a wide range of issues: democracy at the supranational level, the reform of EU policies, as well as institutions. The empirical analysis relies on a citizen survey and on an MEP survey conducted in the framework of the RECONNECT project. We evidence that the level of congruence varies across issues and that it is the representatives, not the citizens, who drive polarization. journal_title: >- European Political Science citation_full: >- Camille Kelbel, Julien Navarro, Marie Neihouser.
